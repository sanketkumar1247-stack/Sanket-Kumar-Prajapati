June 2026 Scopus Q1 The International Spectator

Regulatory Ecosystem Norm Entrepreneurship in the Digital Sphere: EU Governance Frameworks and the Global Digital Order

Sanket Kumar Prajapati

The International Spectator, Vol. 61, No. 2 (Taylor & Francis). doi:10.1080/03932729.2026.2684983

Read Online ↗ Download PDF ↓
View Abstract

This article analyzes the role of the European Union as a norm entrepreneur in global digital governance. Tracing the emergence of the Digital Services Act (DSA) and Digital Markets Act (DMA), it demonstrates how the EU utilizes its regulatory ecosystem to set global benchmarks for platform accountability, data sovereignty, and market contestability. It argues that the Brussels Effect operates through constructivist norm diffusion mechanisms, shaping the regulatory preferences of emerging digital markets like India while establishing new paradigms of democratic digital governance that contest dominant geopolitical models of techno-hegemony.

DOI: 10.1080/03932729.2026.2684983

Conference Papers

November 2025 International Conference Jean Monnet Chair, JNU

Cyber Security as a Strategic Domain of India–Europe Cooperation

Sanket Kumar Prajapati

Presented at the International Conference organized by the Jean Monnet Chair, JNU & MSCW, New Delhi.

View Summary

This paper examines whether shared democratic credentials provide a sufficiently robust foundation for establishing common global cyber norms between India and European actors. Evaluating common cybersecurity directives and digital governance policies, the paper maps areas of convergence (such as protecting democratic processes and critical infrastructure) and divergence (data localization requirements and national sovereignty paradigms).

May 2025 Young Scholars Conference CWANA, JNU

The Politics of Border Security: Evaluating EU Cooperation with Libya and Tunisia

Sanket Kumar Prajapati

Presented at the XI Young Scholars Conference, Centre for West Asian Studies (CWANA), Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi.

View Summary

An empirical and theoretical analysis of the externalization of EU borders. Through funding, technological surveillance, and local coastguard training partnerships in Libya and Tunisia, the EU shifts the burden of migration control outside its geographic perimeter, posing complex humanitarian and sovereignty questions for North African states.

May 2025 Jean Monnet Conference JNU & GGSIPU

Multiculturalism vs. Assimilation: Identity Negotiation among Indian Migrants in the UK and Germany

Sanket Kumar Prajapati

Presented at the International Conference organized by the Jean Monnet Chair, JNU & GGSIPU, New Delhi.

View Summary

A comparative policy study of integration models in Western Europe. Tracking second-generation Indian diaspora members across the United Kingdom (multiculturalism framework) and Germany (assimilationist elements), the paper examines how domestic integration policy landscapes shape identity negotiation and cultural preservation.
