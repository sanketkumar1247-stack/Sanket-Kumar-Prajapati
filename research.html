Current Research

My primary research program explores the intersection of **constructivist international relations theory, global technology regulation, and digital sovereignty**.

In particular, I study how the European Union acts as a "regulatory norm entrepreneur" in the digital sphere, shaping policies on data privacy, artificial intelligence, and platform accountability (e.g., GDPR, Digital Services Act, and AI Act). I analyze the transmission channels of this "Brussels Effect" to emerging economies, focusing particularly on how India negotiates, adopts, or contests these standards to expand its own spaces of technological sovereignty.

Current Projects

Doctoral Monograph Project

Regulatory Diffusion and Digital Sovereignty: Mapping India’s Engagement with the Brussels Effect

This project models the structural mechanisms through which EU digital directives (DSA, DMA, AI Act) influence domestic legislative landscapes in India. It maps the constructivist interplay between international norm emulation and sovereign strategic autonomy.

In Progress · Centre for European Studies, JNU

Comparative Policy Study

The Geopolitics of Trade and Technology Councils: A Comparative Analysis of US-EU and India-EU TTCs

A rigorous policy comparison tracing the institutional density, strategic priorities, and legislative outcomes of the EU's Technology Councils with Washington and New Delhi, focusing particularly on critical materials and semiconductor cooperation.

Drafting Phase · Working Group on Tech Diplomacy

Research Methods

My methodological toolkit bridges **Qualitative Constructivist Analysis** and **Comparative Policy Mapping**.

  • Process Tracing: Reconstructing the precise pathways of legislative adoption and norm domestication in emerging technology bills.
  • Discourse Analysis: Investigating legislative testimonies, government white papers, and corporate stakeholder submissions to identify competing interpretations of digital sovereignty.
  • Comparative Case Study Designs: Structuring most-similar and most-different system designs (MSD/MDD) to trace variations in how emerging powers implement technical standards.

Compiled Datasets & Registries

Curated Database

India-EU Regulatory Convergence Registry (IE-RCR)

A structured registry mapping sections of Indian digital bills (such as the Digital Personal Data Protection Act and draft Digital India Act) to corresponding provisions in EU directives (GDPR, DSA, DMA). Contains textual mappings, semantic similarities, and detailed notes on localized adaptations.

Database version 1.2 · Access available upon request

Geopolitical Network Map

Global Minilateral Tech Agreements Registry (2018–2026)

An exhaustive list of bilaterally and multilaterally negotiated technical alliances, technology councils, critical supply-chain corridors, and semiconductor pacts signed by Indo-Pacific states.

Dataset compiled for "Emerging Era of Minilateralism" book
